What If Nature Held The Secret To Save Humanity | Can Nature Solve Our Biggest Problems.

Biomimicry also known as biomimetic or bio-inspired design, is an innovative approach that involves using nature and its element as inspiration for designing and developing solutions, products, and technologies. This practice has been around since the 1960s and has gained significant attention in recent years due to its potential to address various sustainability challenges. The word "biomimicry" comes from the Ancient Greek words βίος, meaning "life", and μίμησις, meaning "imitation".

Various structures, devices, and materials have been forged for industrial and commercial applications by material scientists, chemists, biologists and mostly engineers and also for designs by artists and architects. There are three main principles of biomimicry;

  • Nature as a model

    Biomimicry emphasizes observing and understanding natural system, such as flight of birds, the structure of leaves or the behavior of ants.

  • Nature as measure

    It uses ecological principles as a yard stick for sustainability, ensuring that human innovations does not harm the environment.

  • Nature as mentor

    Biomimicry seeks to learn from natures 3.8 billion years of evolutionary success recognizing that life has already solve many of the challenges we faced today.

Biomimicry has various applications across different fields, because of the diversity and the complexity of biological systems, the number of features that might be emulated is large. The applications of biomimicry include;

  • Locomotion and Transportation

    Biomimicry has significantly impacted the field of locomotion, inspiring engineers to develop more efficiently and innovative modes of transportation by observing and mimicking the movement of animals Here are some examples:

    • Birds flight

      The study of bird flight has been instrumental in the development of airplanes by observing how birds generate lift, control their flight paths, and maneuver in the air. Engineers have been able to design more efficient aircraft with improved aerodynamics.
      The wing shape of airplanes is directly inspired by bird wings. The aerodynamics of streamlined design of improved Japanese high speed train Shinkansen 500 series were modelled after the beak of kingfisher bird.

    • Insect movement

      Insect like bees and flies exhibit remarkable agility and maneuverability in flight. By studying their complex wing movement and sensory systems, researchers are developing micro-aerial vehicles (MAVs) and drones that can navigate complex environment with great precision. This has also been used to develop MAVs that can hover and maneuver in tight space.

    • Cheetah speed

      Cheetahs are the fastest land animal, capable of accelerating to high speed in short time, by studying their flexible spines, powerful leg and unique gait, engineers are developing faster and more efficient robot and vehicles.
